# MyHomestay Product Brief

## Executive Summary

MyHomestay is an owner-first, Malaysia-first homestay platform for local operators who need better discovery, trust, and simple owner tools.

The product starts with the reality of how many local homestay owners already work: customers discover them through informal channels, then booking and payment continue over WhatsApp. Rather than forcing owners into a heavy marketplace workflow on day one, MyHomestay should give them a clean, trustworthy listing, a simple owner dashboard, and a public profile link they can share anywhere they already find customers.

The MVP should be free at first to build supply, trust, and usage. Once enough owners and listings exist, MyHomestay can introduce a monthly subscription for premium host tools such as featured listings, booking management, verified owner profile cards, income tracking, analytics, and multi-property support.

## Product Positioning

MyHomestay is a clean, trusted homestay directory and owner toolkit for Malaysia's local stay operators, starting with kampung-modern destinations and expanding across Southeast Asia later.

It should not feel like a generic travel template or an Airbnb clone. The brand should feel minimal, artisan, local, calm, and practical: a modern tool for real homestay owners who want to look credible without becoming platform operators.

## First Customer

The first customer is the homestay owner: small operators, family-run stays, kampung-modern properties, and local admins who manage guest inquiries.

Traveller browsing matters, but the first product should optimize for owner onboarding. If owners can create useful, attractive listings quickly, MyHomestay can generate public inventory and give each owner immediate value through shareable pages.

## Customer Problem

Homestay owners often depend on scattered discovery and sales channels:

- WhatsApp conversations
- Facebook posts and groups
- Google Maps presence
- TikTok or Instagram content
- informal referrals
- manually sent photos, rates, and house rules

This creates friction for both sides. Owners repeat the same details in messages, customers struggle to judge trust and fit, and properties can look less credible than they are. MyHomestay should reduce that friction by giving each owner a clean source of truth for their stay.

## MVP Promise

An owner can create a trustworthy homestay listing in under 10 minutes and share a clean public profile link anywhere.

That listing should help a customer answer the basic booking questions before messaging:

- Where is it?
- How many people can stay?
- What does it look like?
- Is it family-friendly or Muslim-friendly?
- What amenities are available?
- What are the house rules?
- What is the price range?
- Who do I contact?

The customer can then continue directly to the owner or admin on WhatsApp for booking and payment.

## MVP Scope

The free MVP should include:

- owner account
- simple owner onboarding
- 1 to 3 listings per owner
- public listing page
- listing photos
- location area, town, and state
- WhatsApp booking CTA
- starting price or price range
- guest capacity
- bedroom and bathroom count
- basic amenities
- Muslim-friendly and family-friendly flags
- house rules
- owner or admin display name
- basic owner dashboard
- listing status and basic performance signals

The MVP should not require online payment processing, commission handling, automatic booking confirmation, or full channel-management complexity.

## Product Flow

1. Owner signs up.
2. Owner creates a property listing.
3. Owner adds required stay details and photos.
4. MyHomestay generates a public listing page.
5. Owner shares the page through WhatsApp, Facebook, TikTok, Google Maps posts, or direct messages.
6. Customer views the listing.
7. Customer taps WhatsApp to continue booking and payment directly with the owner.
8. Owner uses a basic dashboard to manage listing completeness and basic performance.

## Commercial Path

MyHomestay should begin free to reduce friction and build supply.

Potential paid subscription features:

- featured listings
- verified online owner profile card
- booking calendar and booking management
- monthly income tracker
- property performance analytics
- multi-property tools
- AI listing helper
- custom owner mini-site or branded profile page

The subscription model should be planned in the architecture, but the MVP should not depend on paid conversion to be useful.

## Brand And Experience Direction

The brand should feel:

- minimal
- artisan
- kampung modern
- trustworthy
- local without being old-fashioned
- smooth for owners
- easy for customers

The owner dashboard should be calm and practical. The public listing should feel credible, clean, and warm. The product should avoid overdecorated travel imagery, generic gradients, and heavy marketplace clutter.

## Strategic Wedge

MyHomestay's first wedge is not global accommodation scale. It is owner credibility and local-fit discovery in Malaysia.

The product should serve operators who are too informal for major booking infrastructure but serious enough to need a better online presence and simple tools.

If the owner listing workflow works in Malaysia, the same model can later extend to nearby Southeast Asian markets with local filters, trust conventions, languages, and booking behaviors.

## Key Assumptions To Validate

- Homestay owners will create listings if onboarding is simple enough.
- WhatsApp handoff is acceptable for MVP booking and payment behavior.
- Owners value a clean public listing even before MyHomestay has marketplace traffic.
- Muslim-friendly, family-friendly, and owner-defined filters are meaningful differentiators in Malaysia.
- Monthly subscription works better than commission for early host monetization.
- Featured placement, verified profile cards, booking tools, and income tracking are valuable enough for paid tiers.

## Success Signals

Early product success should be measured by owner adoption and listing usefulness before revenue:

- owners onboarded
- listings created
- listing completion rate
- public listing shares
- WhatsApp CTA clicks
- repeat owner logins
- owner-reported bookings or inquiry quality
- paid-feature interest after free usage
